Frequently Asked Questions

How long do I have to make a claim? ▼

For most personal injury claims in the UK, you have three years from the date of your injury (or from the date you became aware of your injury) to start court proceedings. However, this deadline can be shorter in some circumstances, and it's crucial to act quickly. Even if you have three years, evidence degrades over time. Witness memories fade. CCTV footage is deleted. We recommend contacting us as soon as possible to protect your claim.

How much will this cost me? ▼

Under our No Win, No Fee agreement, you pay us nothing unless we win your case. If we successfully settle or win your claim, our fees come from a portion of your compensation (typically 25-33% of damages recovered, depending on case complexity). You'll never pay upfront costs or face surprise bills. This aligns our interests with yours — we only succeed when you do.

How long does a personal injury claim take? ▼

Most straightforward claims settle within 6-12 months. More complex cases involving ongoing medical treatment or court proceedings may take longer. We push for swift resolution while never compromising the value of your claim. We won't accept a lowball offer just to close your case quickly. We'll keep you updated on progress at every stage.

What evidence do I need? ▼

Evidence varies depending on your case type, but typically includes: medical records and treatment history, photographs of the accident scene or injuries, witness statements, police reports (for road accidents), employment records (for workplace claims), and proof of financial losses. Don't worry if you don't have everything — we'll help you gather what's needed. Our evidence checklist in your free case review will tell you exactly what to pursue.

Can I switch solicitors if I'm unhappy? ▼

Yes. You have the right to change solicitors at any point. However, you may face issues transferring your file or complications with insurance agreements. We're confident in our service and track record. If you choose us, we'll prove why you made the right decision every step of the way. Communication, transparency, and results are our foundation.

Will my case go to court? ▼

Most claims (around 90%) settle before reaching court through negotiation. However, some cases do require court proceedings to achieve fair compensation. We'll always advise you of this possibility upfront. If court is necessary, we'll prepare thoroughly and represent you confidently. The goal is always fair resolution — whether through settlement or litigation.
