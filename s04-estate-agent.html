Frequently Asked Questions

What are your fees and how do they compare?
+
We operate on a transparent, fixed-fee structure—no hidden costs or surprise charges. Our fees are competitive with premium agents and typically lower than you'd expect for the calibre of service we provide. We'll discuss all costs clearly during your initial valuation. Most sellers find the investment worthwhile given the higher selling prices we consistently achieve.
How long does the selling process typically take?
+
From valuation to completion, most properties sell within 8–12 weeks. However, we've achieved sales in as little as 18 days, whilst some premium properties take longer due to their complexity or buyer pool. Our average is 21 days to the first offer—significantly faster than the national average of 67 days.
Do I need an EPC certificate? What if mine's out of date?
+
Yes, an EPC (Energy Performance Certificate) is required by law when selling. If yours is expired, you'll need a new one. We can arrange this for you quickly and cost-effectively. A valid EPC is essential for listing your property and achieving viewings.
Is staging really necessary? Won't my home sell on its own?
+
Many buyers form their first impression online. Staging and professional photography increase engagement dramatically—typically resulting in more viewings and higher offers. We've found that properties presented beautifully outsell identical properties shown poorly by 15–25%. It's one of the highest-ROI investments you can make in your sale.
What if the buyer is not chain-free? Will that delay completion?
+
Chain sales are common and manageable. We coordinate closely with all parties involved—the buyer's solicitor, their mortgage lender, and any properties in their chain. Delays can happen, but good communication and professional management minimise them. We prioritise chain-free buyers where possible, but won't reject strong offers simply due to a chain.
Can I still accept an offer if it's below my asking price?
+
Absolutely. You retain complete control throughout the selling process. We'll advise on whether an offer represents fair value, but the decision is always yours. Our role is to negotiate on your behalf and provide expert guidance—the final choice is yours alone.
